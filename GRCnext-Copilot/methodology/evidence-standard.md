# Evidence standard

Evidence is the spine of GRCnext™. Credit is earned by evidence, not by
description. This file defines the verification states, the evidence
types, freshness and aging, provenance and the treatment of contradictory
evidence. It is the long form of Sections 2 and 9 of the full prompt.

## The governing rule

No verified evidence, no verified credit.

Unverified representations may receive provisional credit only when they
are clearly labeled as unverified and paired with an evidence-conversion
action that says what evidence would move the claim from provisional to
verified, who owns producing it and by when.

One corollary matters as much as the rule itself. Never equate "not
supplied" with "does not exist." Missing evidence produces an Unknown or
a Not Demonstrated finding, never a finding that the capability is
absent. The Copilot records what it could not verify and asks for it. It
does not infer absence from silence.

## Verification states

Every material claim carries one of six states.

- Verified. Supported by evidence that was reviewed or observed.
- Provisional. Supported only by a representation, a draft or an
  incomplete artifact. Carries a mandatory evidence-conversion action.
- Not demonstrated. Evidence is absent or insufficient.
- Contradicted. Available evidence conflicts and the conflict is not yet
  resolved.
- Refuted. Available evidence affirmatively disproves the claim. Record
  the disproof and the evidence that establishes it.
- Not applicable. There is a supported reason the requirement does not
  apply. An unsupported Not Applicable is treated as Unknown.

A claim that appears unsupported by any evidence and is asserted as fact
is additionally flagged POTENTIAL HALLUCINATION.

## Evidence types

Evidence types, roughly from weaker to stronger for the purpose of
proving execution:

- Interview representation. What someone said. Weakest. Provisional at
  best until corroborated.
- Controlled document. A version-controlled policy, procedure or runbook.
  Proves intent and design, not execution.
- Configuration. A system setting. Proves a state, not that the state was
  reached through a controlled process.
- Contract. Proves rights and obligations, not that they were exercised.
- System record. A record generated by a system in the ordinary course.
- Log. A time-stamped record of events.
- Ticket. A record of work requested and performed.
- Observed test. A test watched by the assessor.
- Exercise record. The captured output of a structured exercise.
- Production event. Evidence from a real invocation in production.
- Independent assurance result. An external, independent verification.
  Strongest for confidence.

Prefer evidence of actual execution over statements of intended
procedure. A runbook plus an interview is design evidence. A dated
exercise record with timing across the execution clock is execution
evidence. Only the second supports a Demonstrated readiness decision.

## Freshness and aging

Evidence decays. Each material conclusion states a freshness assessment.

- Assign a freshness period appropriate to the volatility of what the
  evidence proves. A quarterly access review ages faster than a
  structural architecture decision.
- Evidence older than its freshness period is treated as Provisional,
  not Verified, until refreshed.
- Note when the underlying environment changed after the evidence was
  produced. A rehearsal that predates a provider migration no longer
  evidences the current path.
- A capability last exercised long ago against a since-changed dependency
  map is stale by construction, regardless of how thorough the original
  exercise was.

## Provenance

For every material finding, the evidence ledger records where available:

- Evidence ID.
- Claim tested.
- Artifact or observation.
- Evidence type.
- Owner.
- Date or period covered.
- Source location.
- Verification status.
- Applicable service.
- Freshness assessment.
- Contradictions or limitations.
- Resulting credit.
- Required evidence-conversion action.

## Contradictory evidence

When two pieces of evidence conflict, the claim is Contradicted until
resolved. The Copilot does not silently pick the more favorable one.

Resolution order:

1. Prefer execution evidence over description. A log of what happened
   outranks a runbook describing what should happen.
2. Prefer more recent evidence over older evidence for volatile claims.
3. Prefer independent evidence over self-attested evidence.
4. If the conflict cannot be resolved from available material, keep the
   claim Contradicted, lower the evidence confidence and raise an
   evidence-conversion action to obtain a tiebreaker.

A conflict resolved under this order records the surviving finding; a
claim disproved by the governing evidence is Refuted, not left
Contradicted.

A Contradicted claim on a core gate blocks a Demonstrated decision the
same way a Fail or Unknown does, because the organization cannot show
which state is true.

## How evidence sets confidence

Evidence confidence in [`scoring-method.md`](scoring-method.md) is driven
by this standard.

- High requires current, independently verifiable evidence including
  observed or executed performance.
- Moderate tolerates limited testing, some aging or some verification
  gaps.
- Low signals heavy reliance on drafts, representations, assumptions or
  stale evidence.
- Indeterminate applies when evidence is insufficient or contradictory.

The point of the standard is not to withhold credit. It is to make credit
mean the same thing every time, so that a Demonstrated decision is
believable to an auditor, a regulator and a board.
